I am a detail-oriented researcher, engineer, inventor, consultant, and data analyst. Skilled in gathering, analyzing, interpreting, and reporting structured and unstructured data. Experienced writing and proofreading technical documents, creating visualizations, designing and configuring software solutions, providing technical support, and working independently and collaboratively to achieve objectives.

I have a Ph.D. in the Computer Engineering from the University of California, Santa Cruz. I researched and developed a <a href="https://escholarship.org/uc/item/6nq496gw">robust path back-tracing guidance system to help blind or visually impaired people retrace the path taken in indoor environments</a>. I also developed a system to convey real-time public transportation travel-related information to the blind or visually impaired.

I worked at IBM Research conducting research on wireless sensor technologies (Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) beacons, Wi-Fi routers), RGB and depth camera sensors, inertial sensors, and Internet of Things (IoT) computing devices to develop Cloud-based & on-premise enterprise software solutions. Some of the projects include an indoor navigation system, a biometrics authentication system for autonomous vehicles, room occupancy detection using Time-of-Flight (ToF) cameras, and other use cases.

I completed a combined total of 6 internships at Intel (2), Volcano (1), NASA (2), and IBM Research (1). I am passionate about conducting research and using technology to develop solutions with the purpose of helping others and our environment. I advice and assist users with computer inquiries and issues, and I have been learning about Geographic Information System (GIS).
