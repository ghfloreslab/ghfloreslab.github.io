I am a researcher, consultant, inventor, and private tutor, working on time series data and machine learning applications. I was a Ph.D. student in the Computer Vision Lab at the University of California, Santa Cruz, advised by <a href="https://users.soe.ucsc.edu/~manduchi/">Prof. Roberto Manduchi</a>. My research focused on developing a <a href="https://escholarship.org/uc/item/6nq496gw">robust path back-tracing guidance system for blind people</a> using inertial sensors. In particular, I developed a system to help blind people retrace the path taken in indoor environments. 

Previously I did research in developing an indoor localization system using Bluetooth Low Energy beacons and Ultra-wide band sensors, and developed a deep learning model for face recognition. I have completed several internships at various tech companies and at NASA Ames Research center. I am passionate about developing algorithms for medicine, and designing and building software systems for assistive technology and teaching purposes.
