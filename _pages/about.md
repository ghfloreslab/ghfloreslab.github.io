I am a detail-oriented researcher, engineer, inventor, consultant, and data analyst. Skilled in collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and reporting structured and unstructured data. Experienced writing and proofreading technical documents, creating visualizations, designing and configuring software solutions, providing technical support, and working independently and collaboratively to achieve objectives.

I have a Ph.D. in the Computer Engineering from the University of California, Santa Cruz. I researched and developed a <a href="https://escholarship.org/uc/item/6nq496gw">robust path back-tracing guidance system</a> to help blind or visually impaired people retrace the path taken in indoor environments. I also developed a system to convey real-time travel-related information when blind or visually impaired people use public transportation.

I worked at IBM Research as a Research Staff Member conducting research and experiments to investigate on wireless sensor technologies (e.g., Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) beacons, Wi-Fi routers), RGB and depth camera sensors, inertial sensors, and Internet of Things (IoT) computing devices to develop Cloud-based & on-premise enterprise software solutions. Some of the projects I worked on include an indoor navigation system, a biometrics authentication system for autonomous vehicles, aroom occupancy detection system using Time-of-Flight (ToF) cameras, and other sensor-based use cases.

I completed a combined total of 6 internships at Intel (2), Volcano (1), NASA (2), and IBM Research (1). I am passionate about conducting research and using technology to develop solutions with the purpose of helping others and our environment. I advice and assist users with computer inquiries and issues, and I have been learning about Geographic Information System (GIS).
